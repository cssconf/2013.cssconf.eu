Dedicated to all people who love and write CSS

In the late days of 2013’s summer, CSSconf.eu will unite the CSS community in Berlin. This is your chance to meet top-notch engineers, world-class speakers, great web designers and all people who care about CSS. With this one-day, one-track conference, CSSconf.eu aims to continue what CSSconf US started earlier this year in Florida.

Be a part of it, join us in Berlin!

Date

CSSconf.eu will happen on September 13 2013 in Berlin and offer one day packed with promising talks and time to spend with great people.
For all details, see our schedule

Venue

The historical building of Villa Elisabeth, a quiet and relaxed gem hidden creates the perfect setting for a great day: hidden in a small park, located in the heart of Berlin and very close to hotels, nightlife, restaurants and the buzzing Mitte district. By the way: On the day of our event, drones will be flying in the adjacent church as part of the NodeCopter hackathon.
Villa Elisabeth | Invalidenstr. 3 | 10115 Berlin

How to get there: Travel and Transport

Getting to Berlin Centre from the Airports

Berlin-Tegel (TXL)

By bus: We recommend choosing Jet Express Bus TXL to Alexanderplatz, from there Villa Elisabeth is only two subway stops away (direction U Osloer Straße until U Rosenthaler Platz).
Location map with bus stops

Bus tickets: You can buy the ticket for your ride to Berlin Centre at a ticket machine or in the bus (just talk to the bus driver), your perfect ticket is Berlin AB. It’s 2.60 EUR and valid for 2 hours and all means of public transport.

By taxi: A taxi ride from TXL to Berlin centre is about 20 EUR.

Berlin-Schönefeld (SXF)

By train: We recommend choosing regional trains RE7 and RB14 (Airport Express) and changing at Berlin-Alexanderplatz, from there Villa Elisabeth is only two subway stops away (direction U Osloer Straße until U Rosenthaler Platz).
You can also take the S-Bahn (S9) and change at Ostkreuz to S5 or S75 (direction Westkreuz or Berlin-Spandau).
Location map with public transport

Public transport tickets: Ticket machines can be found in the station and on the platforms. As the airport is located in fare zone C, you’ll have to buy a ticket Berlin ABC for your ride to Berlin Centre, it’s 3.20 EUR.

By taxi: A taxi ride from SXF to Berlin centre is about 50 EUR.

Public transport in the city

Getting to the venue

The venue is located in the center of Berlin, and is easy to access by public transport:

  • Subway U8 Rosenthaler Platz + 5 minute walk
  • S-Bahn Nordbahnhof + 8 minutes walk
  • Tram M8 and 12 Brunnenstraße/Invalidenstraße + 1 minute walk

Download the subway map (PDF) and the tram map (PDF) for more information.

Public transport: Apps

There’s also various mobile public transport apps that help you get to navigate Berlin:

  • Web: Berlin Public Transport Web App
  • iOS: Fahrinfo Berlin (free), DB Navigator (free), iFahrinfo (0.99 EUR)
  • Android: Öffi Public Transport Buddy (free), Fahrinfo Mobil (free), DB Navigator (free)

Just add Invalidenstr. 3, Berlin as your destination and these nice little tools will help you find your way to the conference venue.

Getting around by Car

Parking

As in Berlin Centre it’s usually hard to find free parking space (and unfortunately, they’re not cheap either), we recommend public transport. Some hotels offer free car parks during your stay, just ask when booking. Here’s a list of all covered car parks (scroll down to Stadtmitte, these are closest to the venue).

Car Sharing

There’s various suppliers for car sharing in Berlin giving you the opportunity to pick cars (they have an app for that) and drop them off (mostly) wherever you want to. The best-known are DriveNow, Car2Go and Multicity. Please mind that the registration process may take some time and all companies require registration fees, so it may be only worth your consideration if you’re planning a longer stay in town.
